<p><p>The Cashmere Knitting Yarn market primarily consists of two types: carded and combed yarns. Carded yarns are produced by carding raw cashmere fibers, resulting in a softer, thicker texture suitable for casual garments. In contrast, combed yarns undergo an additional process that aligns the fibers, creating a finer, smoother, and stronger yarn preferred for high-quality, luxurious knitwear. Both types cater to different segments of the market, with carded yarns focusing on warmth and comfort, while combed yarns emphasize durability and elegance.</p></p>
