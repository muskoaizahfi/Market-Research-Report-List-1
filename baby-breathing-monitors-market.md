<p><p>The baby breathing monitors market comprises two primary types: wearable and non-contact devices. Wearable monitors are typically attached to the baby's clothing or placed on the skin, providing real-time tracking and alerts for abnormal breathing patterns. In contrast, non-contact monitors employ sensors to monitor the baby's breathing from a distance, often utilizing technologies like infrared or radar. Both types aim to enhance infant safety by offering parents peace of mind regarding their child's respiratory health during sleep.</p></p>
