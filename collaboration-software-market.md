<p><p>Collaboration software is available in two primary market types: on-premise and cloud-based solutions. On-premise software is installed locally on a company’s servers and offers enhanced control and security but requires significant maintenance and upfront costs. In contrast, cloud-based collaboration software is hosted on external servers, allowing for easier scalability, remote access, and lower initial investment. Cloud solutions typically offer subscription models, facilitating regular updates and collaboration across geographically dispersed teams, enhancing productivity and flexibility.</p></p>
